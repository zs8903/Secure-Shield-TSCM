Guidance

Operating habits that
actually move the needle.

A few of the practices we see separate well-protected family offices from the ones that end up in a forensic report.

Habits we recommend DO

  • Treat physical, cyber, and human risk as one program with one owner.
  • Require dual approval and call-back verification on every wire and identity change.
  • Use phishing-resistant MFA (hardware keys) on principal accounts.
  • Segment home networks: family, staff, guest, IoT each isolated.
  • Run quarterly tabletop drills with the principal and household.
  • Re-vet vendors annually — they are the most common breach vector.
  • Keep an incident retainer with counsel, IR firm, and PR pre-signed.

Common mistakes DON'T

  • Don't rely on SMS-based MFA — SIM-swap fraud defeats it.
  • Don't let household staff and family share the same Wi-Fi or accounts.
  • Don't store passwords, wire instructions, or scans in personal email.
  • Don't accept urgent wire changes by email — even from known senders.
  • Don't post real-time location or itinerary on social or in group chats.
  • Don't let vendors keep standing access after their engagement ends.
  • Don't treat security as an IT line item — it isn't.
